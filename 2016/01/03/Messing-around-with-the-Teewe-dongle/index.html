Messing around with the Teewe dongle

Posted on Jan 3 2016   |   In TechReview   |  

This little device has been making waves in India. I for one, have been using it everyday to watch YouTube, movies and whatnot, for the past 5 months. A true competitor to Google’s Chromecast, it can also play local files from your computer on to your TV. There is an app for Android, Iphone, Windows Phone, Windows, Mac and Linux too.

And I went all fanboy and requested for features it was missing in my phone app.


Still waiting for the features… Maybe I should get an Android. :)

Specs for nerds

Dual-core ARM Cortex-A9 processor clocked at 1.6GHz
Quad-core GPU
1GB RAM
Wi-Fi 802.11 b/g/n
Runs Android 4.2.2

Its snooping time

I fired up wireshark and noticed that teewe was sending out “Hey, I am here!” packets on a multicast address to notify other devices.

So, I went to YouTube and played a video through Teewe. The chrome plugin and the device opened up a websocket connection and were chatting away merrily. There were pings and pongs and device status messages. :)
But on checking fiddler, I was in for a mild shock. Apparently, they are sending the video URL’s I watch to their server.

On closer inspection, it was just a counter API, probably for finding out popular content that people are watching. No PII here. Added a host entry to block it anyway. ;)

Screen sharing issues

When you share your screen through the chrome plugin, there is a very noticeable lag.
I started snooping around the plugin’s code, which is located at:
C:\Users\Your_User_Name\AppData\Local\Google\Chrome\User Data\Default\Extensions\pmkpinbaonngpkjnkaacegbdpbfjepfa

Saw two things.

  • The audio is turned off (audio : !1)
  • The minFrameRate is set to 60!. Maybe if I reduce the minFrameRate to a lesser value say, 24 (like video files) could it stop the lag? But alas, chrome’s security did not allow me to do this and marked the plugin as corrupted. Arrrggghhh
    Anyway, I don’t use this feature at all. Not a big deal-breaker.

Overall, an outstanding device for the price.
